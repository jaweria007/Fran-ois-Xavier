# ABOUT THIS PROJECT

Project Name:
François-Xavier Cornuot – Premium Law Firm Website

Project Objective:
Design and develop a high-end, conversion-focused website for François-Xavier Cornuot, a Doctor of Public Law and attorney specializing in Public Law and Immigration & Nationality Law.

The website must communicate authority, precision, trust, expertise, and prestige while maintaining a modern and sophisticated digital experience.

Design Philosophy:
This is not a typical law firm website.

The visual direction should feel:

* Refined
* Prestigious
* Institutional
* Architectural
* Minimalist
* Timeless
* Editorial
* Premium

The experience should resemble the quality of a luxury architecture studio, private wealth firm, or elite legal practice.

Source Materials:

1. Site.pdf

* Primary project specification
* Defines content, structure, sections, CTAs, imagery, references, and requirements
* Must be followed completely

2. Brand Guidelines Website
   https://cornuot-charte-graphique.fr/

* Defines overall brand personality

Core Principle:

Site.pdf defines WHAT to build.
Brand Guidelines define HOW it should look.

Website Goals:

* Establish immediate credibility
* Showcase legal expertise
* Communicate professionalism
* Increase consultation inquiries
* Build trust with prospective clients
* Present complex legal services in a clear and elegant manner

Target Audience:

* Public institutions
* Local authorities
* Foreign nationals
* Individuals facing administrative disputes
* Professionals requiring legal guidance
* Clients seeking premium legal representation

User Experience Requirements:

* Clean navigation
* Strong visual hierarchy
* Excellent readability
* Premium typography
* Responsive design
* Smooth animations
* Fast loading experience
* Accessible layout structure

Expected Outcome:
A world-class luxury law firm website that feels bespoke, authoritative, elegant, and meticulously crafted, while remaining fully aligned with the Cornuot brand identity and all specifications provided in Site.pdf.
